15.05 Indigenous Names

Indigenous geographical names require special attention to accuracy, cultural sensitivity, and contemporary usage.

Guiding Principles

ü§ù Respect and Recognition

Indigenous names reflect deep cultural connections to the land and should be used with respect and accuracy.

‚ú® Current Usage

üìö Authoritative Sources

Consult Indigenous language authorities and community preferences for proper spelling and usage.

Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

‚úì Best Practices

  • Include traditional names alongside colonial names when appropriate
  • Use current preferred spellings and pronunciations
  • Acknowledge traditional territories in appropriate contexts
  • Consult with Indigenous communities when possible
  • Respect the cultural significance of place names

‚úó Practices to Avoid

  • Assuming historical names are acceptable
  • Treating Indigenous names as merely alternative
  • Using names without understanding their significance
  • Appropriating sacred or ceremonial names
