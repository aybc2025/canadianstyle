10.01 Introduction

Professional standards for government correspondence and communications

Government Correspondence Standards

All government correspondence must reflect professionalism, clarity, and adherence to Federal Identity Program guidelines.

Core Principles

  • Clarity: Use clear, concise language that serves the public
  • Consistency: Follow established templates and formats
  • Accessibility: Ensure communications are accessible to all Canadians
  • Bilingual requirements: Consider official language obligations

Types of Government Correspondence

Formal letters: Official communications to external parties
Memorandums: Internal communications within government
Electronic correspondence: Email and digital communications
Transmittal letters: Documents accompanying reports or materials

10.02 Business Letters

Format and structure for formal business correspondence

Business Letter Components

  1. Letterhead: Official government letterhead
  2. Date: Full date written out
  3. Inside address: Recipient's complete address
  4. Salutation: Appropriate greeting
  5. Body: Clear, organized content
  6. Complimentary close: Professional closing
  7. Signature: Handwritten signature and typed name
  8. Reference information: File numbers, etc.

Sample Business Letter Format

[Government of Canada Letterhead] March 15, 2024 Ms. Sarah Johnson Director, Policy Development Canadian Association of Municipalities 24 Clarence Street Ottawa, ON K1N 5P3 Dear Ms. Johnson: Subject: Municipal Infrastructure Funding Guidelines Thank you for your letter of March 1, 2024, regarding the new municipal infrastructure funding guidelines. [Body paragraphs...] I trust this information addresses your concerns. Please contact me if you require further clarification. Yours sincerely, [Signature] Michael Thompson Director, Infrastructure Policy Infrastructure Canada MT/sb File: 12345-67890

10.04 Memorandums

Internal government communication format and conventions

Memorandum Format

  • Header: "MEMORANDUM" or department letterhead
  • To: Recipient's name and title
  • From: Sender's name and title
  • Date: Full date
  • Subject: Clear, descriptive subject line
  • Body: Direct, organized content
  • Attachments: List if applicable

Sample Memorandum

10.08 Electronic Correspondence

Email and digital communication standards

Email Best Practices

  • Subject line: Clear, specific, and informative
  • Greeting: Professional salutation
  • Body: Concise, organized content
  • Closing: Professional sign-off with contact information
  • Attachments: Referenced in body, properly named
