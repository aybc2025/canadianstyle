10.01 Introduction

üíº Professional Communication

Effective correspondence creates positive impressions and facilitates clear communication in professional settings.

Types of Professional Correspondence

üìß Business Letters

Formal external communication with clients, partners, and other organizations

  • Official letterhead
  • Formal tone and structure
  • Complete address information
  • Professional signatures

üìù Memorandums

Internal communication within an organization

  • Standardized format
  • Clear subject lines
  • Concise, action-oriented
  • Appropriate distribution

üì® Email Communication

Digital correspondence for various purposes

  • Professional subject lines
  • Appropriate tone
  • Clear signatures
  • Proper etiquette

Universal Principles

  • Clarity: Make your purpose immediately clear
  • Conciseness: Respect your reader's time
  • Courtesy: Maintain professional tone
  • Correctness: Ensure accuracy in all details
  • Completeness: Include all necessary information

Salutations and Closings

Professional Salutations

  • Formal: Dear Mr./Ms./Dr. [Last Name]:
  • Unknown name: Dear Sir or Madam:
  • Position known: Dear Hiring Manager:
  • Less formal: Dear [First Name]:

Professional Closings

  • Formal: Yours truly, Respectfully yours
  • Standard: Sincerely, Sincerely yours
  • Warm: Best regards, Kind regards
  • Informal: Best wishes, Cordially

10.03 Memorandums

Memorandums (memos) are internal documents that communicate information efficiently within organizations.

Standard Memo Format

MEMORANDUM
TO: All Department Managers
FROM: Sarah Chen, Director of Operations
DATE: March 15, 2024
SUBJECT: Updated Remote Work Policy

This memo outlines the updated remote work policy effective April 1, 2024.

Key Changes:

  • Increased flexibility for remote work arrangements
  • New approval process for hybrid schedules
  • Updated equipment and technology guidelines

Please review the attached policy document and discuss with your teams by March 30.

Memo Writing Guidelines

üéØ Subject Lines

  • Be specific and descriptive
  • Use action words when appropriate
  • Keep under 10 words when possible
  • Avoid vague terms like "Important"

üìù Content Structure

  • Start with purpose/main message
  • Use headings for longer memos
  • Include action items clearly
  • End with next steps or deadlines

üì§ Distribution

  • Include only necessary recipients
  • Use "cc:" for information only
  • Consider confidentiality levels
  • Follow organizational protocols

10.04 Formatting

Consistent formatting enhances readability and maintains professional standards across all correspondence.

General Formatting Standards

üìÑ Page Layout

  • Margins: 2.5 cm (1 inch) all sides
  • Font: Times New Roman 12pt or Arial 11pt
  • Line spacing: Single space within paragraphs
  • Paragraph spacing: Double space between paragraphs

üî§ Typography

  • Headings: Bold, larger font size
  • Emphasis: Use bold or italics sparingly
  • Lists: Consistent bullet points or numbering
  • Alignment: Left-aligned for body text

üìù Content Organization

  • Paragraphs: One main idea per paragraph
  • Length: 3-5 sentences per paragraph
  • Transitions: Smooth flow between ideas
  • White space: Use strategically for readability

Digital Communication Etiquette

üìù Writing Style

  • Use professional tone, even in brief messages
  • Avoid ALL CAPS (appears as shouting)
  • Use proper grammar and spelling
  • Keep messages concise and scannable

üì§ Sending Practices

  • Use "Reply All" judiciously
  • Include clear call-to-action when needed
  • Proofread before sending
  • Consider time zones for urgent messages

üîí Security & Privacy

  • Be cautious with sensitive information
  • Use appropriate distribution lists
  • Follow organizational email policies
  • Consider classification levels

Response Expectations

‚ö° Urgent (Same Day)

  • Time-sensitive decisions
  • Crisis communications
  • Last-minute meeting changes

üìÖ Standard (1-2 Business Days)

  • Regular business inquiries
  • Project updates
  • Non-urgent requests

üìã Extended (3-5 Business Days)

  • Complex research requests
  • Policy development questions
  • Detailed analysis requirements
