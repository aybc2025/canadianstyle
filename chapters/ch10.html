10.01 Introduction

Business correspondence remains a cornerstone of professional communication. This chapter covers the standard formats for letters and memorandums in Canadian government and business contexts.

General Principles

  • Clarity: State your purpose clearly
  • Conciseness: Respect the reader's time
  • Courtesy: Maintain a professional tone
  • Correctness: Ensure accuracy in all details
  • Completeness: Include all necessary information

Modern Considerations

  • Email has replaced many formal letters
  • Digital signatures are increasingly common
  • Templates ensure consistency
  • Accessibility requirements apply
Key Point

Even in digital formats, the principles of good business correspondence remain unchanged: be clear, concise, and courteous.

10.02 Block Style

Block style is the standard format for business letters in Canada. All elements begin at the left margin with no indentation.

Block Style Elements

Letterhead
Date
Inside Address
Salutation:
Body paragraphs (no indentation)
Body paragraphs separated by blank line
Complimentary close,
Signature
Typed name
Title

Spacing Guidelines

  • Single space within elements
  • Double space between elements
  • No paragraph indentation
  • One blank line between paragraphs
  • Four lines for signature space

Modified Block Style

Some organizations use modified block style where:

  • Date aligns with right margin
  • Complimentary close and signature block center or align right
  • Paragraphs may be indented
Canadian Government Standard: Use full block style for all official correspondence unless otherwise specified.

10.05 Date

The date indicates when the letter was written and becomes part of the official record.

Date Formats

Preferred Canadian Format

March 15, 2024

Also Acceptable

15 March 2024

Government/Military

2024-03-15 (ISO format)

Date Placement

  • Block style: Flush left
  • Modified block: Aligned right or centered
  • Position: 2-3 lines below letterhead

Never Use

⚠️ Avoid These Formats

  • 03/15/24 (ambiguous)
  • 15/03/24 (ambiguous)
  • March 15th, 2024 (no ordinal)
  • 15-03-24 (use full year)

Special Situations

  • Delayed sending: Use actual mailing date
  • Effective dates: Specify in the body
  • Foreign correspondence: Include day of week if helpful

10.06 Inside Address

The inside address shows exactly where the letter is being sent and to whom.

Standard Format

Ms. Jennifer Smith

Director of Communications

Environment and Climate Change Canada

200 Sacré-Cœur Boulevard

Gatineau QC K1A 0H3

Elements in Order

  1. Recipient's name: Include courtesy title
  2. Title/Position: If known
  3. Department/Division: If applicable
  4. Organization name: Full official name
  5. Street address: Include suite/floor
  6. City, Province, Postal Code: Two spaces before postal code

Courtesy Titles

Use For Notes
Mr. Men Standard
Ms. Women Preferred default
Mrs. Married women If preferred by recipient
Dr. PhD, MD, etc. Takes precedence
Prof. Professors Academic context

When Name Unknown

Options

Director of Human Resources

Customer Service Department

Attention: Accounts Payable

Provincial Abbreviations

Use two-letter postal abbreviations without periods:

ON - Ontario QC - Quebec BC - British Columbia AB - Alberta MB - Manitoba SK - Saskatchewan NS - Nova Scotia NB - New Brunswick PE - Prince Edward Island NL - Newfoundland and Labrador YT - Yukon NT - Northwest Territories NU - Nunavut

10.12 Salutation

The salutation sets the tone for your letter. Choose appropriately based on your relationship with the recipient.

Standard Salutations

When You Know the Name

  • Dear Ms. Smith:
  • Dear Dr. Johnson:
  • Dear Professor Lee:
  • Dear Mr. Prime Minister: (high officials)

When Name Unknown

  • Dear Director:
  • Dear Hiring Manager:
  • Dear Customer Service:
  • To Whom It May Concern: (avoid if possible)

Less Formal

  • Dear Jennifer: (if on first-name basis)
  • Hello Jennifer: (very informal)

Punctuation

  • Business letters: Use colon (:)
  • Personal letters: Use comma (,)
  • Email: Either is acceptable

Group Salutations

Addressing Groups

Dear Board Members:

Dear Colleagues:

Dear Students:

Ladies and Gentlemen: (formal)

Avoid These

⚠️ Outdated or Problematic

  • Dear Sir/Madam (gendered assumptions)
  • Gentlemen (unless all male)
  • Dear Ms. Jennifer Smith (don't use first and last)
  • To Whom It May Concern (too impersonal)

Special Titles

For government officials and dignitaries:

  • Dear Prime Minister:
  • Dear Minister:
  • Dear Senator Smith:
  • Your Excellency: (ambassadors)
  • Dear Justice Brown: (judges)

10.17 Complimentary Close

The complimentary close should match the tone of your salutation and letter.

Standard Closes

Formal

  • Respectfully,
  • Respectfully yours,
  • Very truly yours,

Standard Business

  • Sincerely,
  • Sincerely yours,
  • Yours sincerely,
  • Yours truly,

Less Formal

  • Best regards,
  • Kind regards,
  • Regards,
  • Best wishes,

Informal

  • Best,
  • All the best,
  • Cheers, (very casual)

Formatting

  • Capitalize only the first word
  • Always use a comma
  • Leave 3-4 blank lines for signature
  • Type name below signature space
  • Include title on next line

Signature Block

Sincerely,




Jennifer Smith

Director of Communications

Additional Elements

  • Enclosure notation: Enclosure or Enc.
  • Copy notation: cc: John Doe
  • Attachment: Attachment: Annual Report
  • Postscript: P.S. (use sparingly)
Email Tip: In email, your signature block can include contact information, but keep it concise—usually 4-5 lines maximum.

10.23 Memorandums

Memorandums (memos) are internal documents used for communication within organizations.

Standard Memo Format

MEMORANDUM


TO: All Staff

FROM: Jennifer Smith, Director

DATE: March 15, 2024

SUBJECT: New Policy Implementation

Body text begins here without salutation...

Memo Elements

  • TO: Recipients (names, titles, or groups)
  • FROM: Your name and title
  • DATE: Full date
  • SUBJECT: Clear, specific subject line
  • CC: Others receiving copies (optional)

Key Differences from Letters

Element Letter Memo
Audience External Internal
Salutation Required None
Close Required None
Signature Full signature Initials only
Tone More formal Direct, brief

Writing Effective Memos

  • Purpose first: State your main point immediately
  • Be concise: One page is ideal
  • Use headings: For longer memos
  • Action items: Clearly state what you need
  • Deadline: Include if applicable

Digital Memos

Email has largely replaced paper memos, but the format principles remain:

  • Clear subject line
  • Direct opening
  • Organized content
  • Specific action items
  • Professional tone
Remember

Memos are action-oriented. Always be clear about what you want the reader to know or do.
