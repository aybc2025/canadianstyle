10.01 Introduction

Professional correspondence reflects your organization's image and competence. Clear, well-formatted letters and memorandums help achieve your communication goals and maintain positive relationships with colleagues, clients, and stakeholders.

Fundamental Principles

  • Clarity: Say what you need to say clearly and directly
  • Conciseness: Use only the words you need
  • Courtesy: Maintain a respectful, professional tone
  • Correctness: Ensure accuracy in facts, spelling, and grammar

Essential Elements

Every piece of correspondence should clearly show:

  • Who is sending the message
  • Who should receive it
  • When it was written or sent
  • What it's about
  • What follow-up is required

💡 Modern Context

While email has largely replaced traditional letters, the principles of clear, professional communication remain the same across all formats.

10.02 Business Letters

Standard Business Letter Format

The Government of Canada recommends the block style for administrative correspondence:

Block Style Elements (in order)

  1. Letterhead (if available)
  2. Date
  3. Delivery notation (if applicable)
  4. Reference line (if applicable)
  5. Inside address
  6. Attention line (if applicable)
  7. Salutation
  8. Subject line (if applicable)
  9. Body of letter
  10. Complimentary close
  11. Signature
  12. Reference initials
  13. Enclosure notation
  14. Copy notation

Sample Business Letter Layout

GOVERNMENT OF CANADA

Department of Innovation, Science and Economic Development


2024-03-15


Ms. Sarah Johnson

Executive Director

Canadian Technology Association

123 Innovation Drive

Ottawa ON K1A 0A6


Dear Ms. Johnson:


Subject: Digital Innovation Program - Application Deadline


Thank you for your inquiry regarding the Digital Innovation Program...


Sincerely,



Michael Chen

Director, Innovation Programs

Key Formatting Rules

  • All lines begin flush with the left margin
  • Use single spacing within paragraphs
  • Double space between paragraphs and major sections
  • Use standard business letter punctuation (colon after salutation, comma after complimentary close)

10.03 Memorandums

Internal Communication Format

Memorandums are used for internal communication within organizations. They have a standardized format that's more direct than business letters:

Standard Memorandum Elements

  • TO: Recipient name and title
  • FROM: Sender name and title
  • DATE: Date of writing
  • SUBJECT: Clear, specific subject line
  • Body: Main message

Sample Memorandum

MEMORANDUM


TO: All Staff

FROM: Jennifer Martinez, Human Resources Director

DATE: 2024-03-15

SUBJECT: New Flexible Work Policy - Effective April 1, 2024


This memorandum outlines the new flexible work policy that will take effect on April 1, 2024.


Key changes include:

• Option to work from home up to 3 days per week

• Flexible start times between 7:00 a.m. and 10:00 a.m.

• Compressed work week options available


Please review the complete policy document attached and contact HR with any questions.

Memorandum Writing Guidelines

  • Purpose first: State the main point in the opening paragraph
  • Be concise: Use bullet points for lists and action items
  • No salutation: Start directly with the message
  • No signature: The FROM line identifies the author
  • Action required: Clearly state what recipients should do

10.04 Formatting Standards

Page Layout and Typography

Consistent formatting enhances readability and professionalism:

Standard Specifications

  • Paper size: 8.5" × 11" (letter size)
  • Margins: 1" (2.5 cm) on all sides, 1.5" (3.8 cm) left for binding
  • Font: Times New Roman 12pt or equivalent
  • Line spacing: Single space within paragraphs, double between paragraphs
  • Alignment: Left-aligned (block style)

Spacing Guidelines

✓ Standard spacing:

  • 3-6 line spaces between date and inside address
  • 1 line space between inside address and salutation
  • 1 line space between salutation and body
  • 1 line space between paragraphs
  • 1 line space between body and complimentary close
  • 4 line spaces between complimentary close and typed signature

Punctuation in Correspondence

  • Standard punctuation: Colon after salutation, comma after complimentary close
  • Open punctuation: No punctuation after salutation or complimentary close
  • Consistency: Use the same style throughout the document

💡 Digital Considerations

For electronic documents, ensure fonts and formatting display correctly across different systems. Use standard fonts and avoid unusual formatting that may not transfer properly.

10.05 Addresses and Official Languages

Proper Address Formatting

Follow Canada Post standards and official language requirements for addresses:

Address Components (in order)

  1. Recipient name and title
  2. Organization name
  3. Street address
  4. City, province/territory, postal code
  5. Country (if international)

Canadian Address Examples

✓ Government address:

The Honourable Chrystia Freeland
Deputy Prime Minister
House of Commons
Ottawa ON K1A 0A6

✓ Business address:

Ms. Catherine Liu
Chief Executive Officer
Innovation Solutions Inc.
1500 Bank Street, Suite 400
Ottawa ON K1H 1B8

Official Language Considerations

  • Federal government: Provide addresses in both official languages when required
  • Bilingual regions: Consider recipient's language preference
  • Quebec addresses: French is the standard; English may be included
  • Courtesy: Use the recipient's preferred language when known

Bilingual Address Example

✓ Bilingual format (when required):

Mr. Jean Dubois / M. Jean Dubois
Director / Directeur
Canadian Heritage / Patrimoine canadien
Gatineau QC K1A 0M5

💡 Postal Code Format

Canadian postal codes: Use the format "K1A 0A6" with a space between the first three and last three characters. In addresses, use two spaces between province and postal code.

10.06 Signatures and Authorization

Signature Block Components

The signature block provides authorization and identification for the correspondence:

Signature Block Elements

  • Complimentary close: "Sincerely," "Yours truly," etc.
  • Handwritten signature space: 4 line spaces
  • Typed name: Full name of sender
  • Title: Official position or rank
  • Organization: Department or organization name (if not on letterhead)

Signature Block Examples

✓ Standard signature block:

Sincerely,





Patricia Thompson
Assistant Deputy Minister
Policy and Strategic Direction

✓ With enclosures and copies:

Yours truly,





David Kim
Regional Director


Enclosure: Policy Guidelines
c: Deputy Minister
   Communications Director

Reference Notations

  • Enclosures: "Enclosure" or "Enclosures (2)"
  • Attachments: "Attachment" or "Attachments"
  • Copies: "c:" followed by recipients
  • Blind copies: "bc:" (on sender's copy only)
  • Reference initials: Typist's initials in lowercase

💡 Electronic Signatures

In digital documents, electronic signatures may replace handwritten ones. Ensure compliance with your organization's electronic signature policies and any legal requirements.

10.07 Electronic Communication

Email and Digital Correspondence

Electronic communication has largely replaced traditional letters, but professional standards still apply:

Email Best Practices

  • Subject line: Clear, specific, and informative
  • Greeting: Professional salutation appropriate to the relationship
  • Body: Clear, concise message with logical organization
  • Closing: Professional sign-off with contact information
  • Attachments: Reference in the email body and use descriptive file names

Professional Email Structure

✓ Well-structured email:

Subject: Action Required: Budget Proposal Review - Due March 20


Dear Sarah,


I hope this email finds you well. I'm writing to request your review of the attached budget proposal for the Digital Innovation Project.


Key points for your consideration:

• Total budget: $2.5 million over three years

• Staff allocation: 5 FTE positions

• Expected deliverables: See section 4 of the proposal


Please provide your feedback by March 20 so we can finalize the submission for the Treasury Board review.


Thank you for your time and consideration.


Best regards,
Michael Chen
Director, Innovation Programs
michael.chen@canada.ca
(613) 555-0123

Digital Communication Guidelines

  • Tone: Maintain professionalism; avoid casual language in formal communications
  • Length: Keep emails concise; use attachments for detailed information
  • Response time: Acknowledge receipt and set expectations for full response
  • Privacy: Be mindful of confidential information and distribution lists
  • Formatting: Use standard fonts and minimal formatting for compatibility

⚠️ Security Considerations

Be cautious with sensitive information in electronic communications. Use secure systems for confidential government information and follow your organization's IT security policies.

Subject Line Examples

✓ Effective subject lines:

  • Meeting Request: Digital Strategy Discussion - March 25, 2 p.m.
  • Action Required: Contract Review - Deadline March 30
  • FYI: New Procurement Guidelines Effective April 1
  • Follow-up: Budget Meeting Action Items

✗ Poor subject lines:

  • Meeting
  • Quick question
  • Important!
  • FYI
