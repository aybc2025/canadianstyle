10.01 Introduction

Professional correspondence reflects your organization's image and competence. This chapter covers formatting and writing conventions for business letters, memorandums, and electronic communications.

Correspondence Principles

  • Clear, concise, and courteous communication
  • Consistent formatting and layout
  • Appropriate tone for audience and purpose
  • Complete and accurate information
  • Professional appearance

üí° First Impressions Matter

Your correspondence is often the first contact people have with your organization. Make it count with professional formatting and clear, helpful content.

10.02 Business Letters

Letter Components

A standard business letter includes these elements in order:

‚úÖ Letter Structure

  1. Letterhead (organization's contact information)
  2. Date (spelled out: March 15, 2024)
  3. Inside address (recipient's name and address)
  4. Salutation (Dear Ms. Johnson:)
  5. Body (main message in paragraphs)
  6. Complimentary close (Sincerely,)
  7. Signature block (name and title)
  8. Enclosures (if applicable)

Salutations

Formal Options

  • Specific person: Dear Ms. Johnson: / Dear Mr. Chen:
  • Unknown gender: Dear A. Johnson:
  • Position known: Dear Director: / Dear Hiring Manager:
  • General: Dear Sir or Madam: / To Whom It May Concern:

Complimentary Closes

‚úÖ Appropriate Closes

Formal: Sincerely, / Yours truly,
Less formal: Best regards, / Kind regards,
Respectful: Respectfully yours,

‚ùå Avoid

Cheers, / Thanks, / TTYL,
Love, / Your friend,

10.03 Memorandums

Memo Format

Memorandums are used for internal communication. They have a standard header format:

‚úÖ Memo Header

MEMORANDUM

TO: All Department Managers
FROM: Sarah Johnson, Director
DATE: March 15, 2024
SUBJECT: New Meeting Room Booking System

Subject Lines

Write clear, specific subject lines that summarize the memo's purpose:

‚úÖ Clear Subjects

Policy Update: Remote Work Guidelines
Deadline Reminder: Budget Submissions Due March 30
Training Opportunity: Customer Service Workshop

‚ùå Vague Subjects

Update
Important Information
FYI

Memo Body

Writing Guidelines

  • Purpose first: State the main point in the opening paragraph
  • Be concise: Use bullet points for lists and action items
  • No salutation: Start directly with the message
  • No signature: The FROM line identifies the author
  • Action required: Clearly state what recipients should do

10.04 Formatting Standards

Page Layout

Standard Specifications

  • Margins: 2.5 cm (1 inch) on all sides
  • Font: 12-point Times New Roman or Arial
  • Line spacing: Single-spaced within paragraphs, double-spaced between
  • Alignment: Left-aligned for most elements
  • Page numbers: Bottom centre or top right, starting on page 2

Spacing Guidelines

‚úÖ Proper Spacing

Between date and inside address: 3-4 lines
Between address and salutation: 2 lines
Between salutation and body: 1 line
Between body and close: 2 lines
Between close and signature: 4 lines for handwritten signature

Special Elements

Additional Notations

  • Enclosures: Enclosure (or Encl.) ‚Äî list attachments
  • Copies: c: (carbon copy) or cc: (courtesy copy)
  • Confidential: Mark at top and bottom of each page
  • Reference numbers: Include file or reference numbers when required

Special Situations

Signing Authority

  • On behalf of: Use when signing for someone else
  • Acting: Include "Acting" in the title when temporarily in role
  • Multiple signatories: Joint approval
  • Copies: "Original signed by"
  • Urgent matters: Telephone confirmation
  • Confidential: Secure handling procedures

10.07 Electronic Communication

Email Standards

Professional email follows similar principles to traditional correspondence:

Email Guidelines

  • Subject line: Clear, specific, and actionable
  • Greeting: Appropriate for relationship and context
  • Body: Concise, well-organized, professional
  • Signature: Include name, title, and contact information
  • Attachments: Reference them in the email body

Subject Line Best Practices

‚úÖ Effective Subjects

Action Required: Budget Review by March 30
Meeting Confirmation: April 15 Project Update
Response Needed: Policy Draft Comments

‚ùå Poor Subjects

Quick question
FYI
Follow up

Email Etiquette

Professional Standards

  • Response time: Acknowledge within 24 hours
  • Reply vs. Reply All: Consider who needs the information
  • Tone: Professional and courteous
  • Formatting: Use paragraphs and bullet points
  • Confidentiality: Mark sensitive information appropriately
