Spacing and Layout Rules

üìè Vertical Spacing

  • Between sections: Double space (two line breaks)
  • Within paragraphs: Single space
  • Between paragraphs: Double space
  • After punctuation: Single space

üìê Margins

  • Standard margins: 1 inch (2.5 cm) on all sides
  • Left alignment: For all text (not centered)
  • Justification: Left-aligned, not fully justified

Date Formatting

‚úÖ Preferred Canadian Format

Month Day, Year March 15, 2024

‚úÖ Acceptable Alternatives

Day Month Year 15 March 2024
Abbreviated Mar. 15, 2024

‚ùå Avoid These Formats

Numerical only 15/03/2024 or 03/15/2024 (ambiguous internationally)
With ordinals March 15th, 2024 (unnecessary in date line)

Paper and Font Guidelines

üìÑ Paper Standards

  • Size: 8.5" √ó 11" (216 √ó 279 mm)
  • Quality: Good quality white paper
  • Weight: 20 lb (75 gsm) minimum

üî§ Typography

  • Font: Professional fonts (Times, Arial, Calibri)
  • Size: 12 point for body text
  • Style: Regular weight, no decorative fonts

üí° Professionalism Tip

Consistency in formatting shows attention to detail. Use the same format throughout your organization for a professional brand image.

10.02 Salutations and Closings

ü§ù Setting the Right Tone

Appropriate salutations and closings establish the proper level of formality for your correspondence.

Salutation Guidelines

üë§ When You Know the Person

Dear Mr. Smith: Male recipient, formal
Dear Ms. Johnson: Female recipient, formal
Dear Dr. Brown: Professional title
Dear Professor Wilson: Academic title

‚ùì When You Don't Know the Person

Dear Sir or Madam: Traditional formal
To Whom It May Concern: General inquiries
Dear Hiring Manager: Specific role unknown person
Dear Customer Service: Department-specific

Title Usage in Salutations

‚úÖ Preferred Practices

  • Ms. for women (unless you know they prefer Mrs.)
  • Use full titles: Doctor, Professor, Reverend
  • Government officials: The Honourable (if applicable)
  • Multiple recipients: Dear Colleagues: or Dear Team:

Examples by Profession

Medical Doctor: Dear Dr. Anderson:
Judge: Dear Justice Thompson:
Minister: Dear Minister (or The Honourable Jane Doe):
Mayor: Dear Mayor Smith:

Complimentary Closings

üé© Most Formal

Respectfully yours, Government officials, very formal
Yours very truly, Very formal business

üëî Standard Formal

Sincerely yours, Most common business closing
Yours truly, Standard business
Sincerely, Slightly less formal

ü§ù Less Formal

Best regards, Professional but warmer
Kind regards, Friendly professional
Cordially, Warm but professional

Punctuation Rules

üìç Salutation Punctuation

‚úÖ Dear Mr. Smith: Colon in business letters
‚ùå Dear Mr. Smith; Never use semicolon
üìß Dear John, Comma acceptable in email

üìç Closing Punctuation

‚úÖ Sincerely yours, Always comma after closing
‚ùå Sincerely yours. Never period

Matching Formality Levels

Formal Combination

"Dear Dr. Thompson:" matches "Respectfully yours,"

Standard Combination

"Dear Ms. Johnson:" matches "Sincerely yours,"

Friendly Professional

"Dear Sarah," matches "Best regards,"

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid Mismatched Formality

Don't mix formal salutations with casual closings or vice versa. "Dear Sir or Madam:" should not end with "Cheers," and "Hi John," should not end with "Respectfully yours."

10.04 Memorandums

üìã Internal Communication

Memorandums provide an efficient format for internal organizational communication.

Standard Memo Format

MEMORANDUM
TO: All Department Staff
FROM: Sarah Johnson, Department Director
DATE: March 15, 2024
SUBJECT: New Office Safety Procedures

Effective immediately, all staff must follow the updated safety procedures outlined below...

Please review the attached guidelines and ensure compliance by April 1, 2024.

If you have questions, please contact the Safety Coordinator at extension 5555.

Memo Header Elements

üìß TO: Line

Purpose: Identifies the recipient(s)

Individual: TO: Dr. John Smith
Group: TO: All Marketing Staff
Multiple: TO: Division Heads (See distribution list)

üë§ FROM: Line

Purpose: Identifies the sender

With title: FROM: Sarah Johnson, Director
With department: FROM: Robert Lee, Human Resources

üìÖ DATE: Line

Purpose: Shows when the memo was written

Full date: DATE: March 15, 2024
Alternative: DATE: 15 March 2024

üìù SUBJECT: Line

Purpose: Clearly states the memo's topic

Specific: SUBJECT: Budget Meeting - April 3, 2024
Clear: SUBJECT: Updated Vacation Policy

Memo Writing Guidelines

üìê Structure

  • Opening: State purpose immediately
  • Body: Provide necessary details
  • Closing: Specify next steps or actions
  • Length: Usually one page or less

‚úçÔ∏è Style

  • Tone: Direct and professional
  • Language: Clear and concise
  • Format: Use headings and bullets for clarity
  • Action items: Make them specific and clear

Types of Memos

üì¢ Announcement Memo

Purpose: Inform staff of changes, events, or news

"New Employee Benefits Program" "Office Relocation Update" "Holiday Schedule 2024"

üìã Directive Memo

Purpose: Provide instructions or establish policy

"Updated Expense Reporting Procedures" "New Security Protocols" "Mandatory Training Requirements"
üìä Report Memo

Purpose: Share information, data, or progress updates

"Quarterly Sales Results" "Project Status Update" "Budget Variance Analysis"
‚ùì Request Memo

Purpose: Ask for information, resources, or action

"Request for Budget Approval" "Need for Additional Staff" "Equipment Replacement Request"

Distribution and Filing

üì§ Distribution Methods

  • Email: Most common for routine memos
  • Print copies: For formal announcements
  • Intranet posting: For organization-wide information
  • Distribution list: Include when multiple recipients

üóÇÔ∏è Record Keeping

  • File copies: Maintain records of important memos
  • Reference numbers: Use for tracking if needed
  • Follow-up: Note any required actions or responses

üí° Effectiveness Tip

A good memo answers: Who needs to know? What do they need to know? When do they need to know it? What action is required? Keep it focused and actionable.

10.06 Electronic Correspondence

üíª Digital Communication

Electronic correspondence requires adapted formatting while maintaining professional standards.

Email Formatting Adaptations

üìß Email Structure

Subject Line: Critical - first impression
Subject: Budget Meeting Rescheduled - New Date: April 15
Greeting: Less formal than letters
Dear Sarah, (comma acceptable in email)
Body: Concise and scannable
Short paragraphs, bullet points for lists
Closing: Professional but briefer
Best regards, / Kind regards, / Sincerely,
Signature: Include contact information
Automatic signature block with phone, title

Subject Line Best Practices

‚úÖ Effective Subject Lines

Action required: ACTION REQUIRED: Expense Reports Due Friday
Meeting: Team Meeting Monday 2 PM - Agenda Attached
Question: Quick Question: Budget Code for Training Materials
FYI: FYI: New Security Procedures - Effective March 20

‚ùå Avoid These Subject Lines

Hi Too vague
URGENT!!! Overused, multiple exclamation marks
(no subject) Missing entirely
Re: Re: Re: Re: Budget Too many forwards

Professional Email Etiquette

‚è∞ Response Time

  • Urgent: Within 2 hours during business hours
  • Standard: Within 24 hours
  • Complex queries: Acknowledge within 24 hours, detailed response as needed
  • Out of office: Set up automatic replies for absences

üë• CC and BCC Usage

  • CC: People who need to know but don't need to respond
  • BCC: Large distribution lists to protect privacy
  • Reply All: Use sparingly - only when everyone needs the response
  • To: People who need to take action or respond

üìé Attachments

  • Mention: Reference attachments in the email body
  • Size: Keep under 10MB; use file sharing for larger files
  • Format: Use widely compatible formats (PDF, Word)
  • Names: Use descriptive file names

Tone and Formality in Email

üé© Formal Email

Subject: Request for Budget Allocation Review

Dear Dr. Thompson:

I am writing to request a review of the budget allocation for the upcoming fiscal year...

I would appreciate the opportunity to discuss this matter at your convenience.

Sincerely,
John Smith
Use for: External clients, senior executives, first contact, formal requests

üëî Professional Email

Subject: Team Meeting - Updated Agenda

Hi Sarah,

I've updated the agenda for Monday's meeting. Please review the attached document and let me know if you have any additions.

Thanks,
John
Use for: Regular colleagues, routine business, established relationships

ü§ù Friendly Professional

Subject: Quick Question on Project Timeline

Hi Mike,

Hope you had a good weekend! Quick question about the project timeline - are we still on track for the March 30 deadline?

Let me know when you get a chance.

Best,
Sarah
Use for: Close colleagues, team members, informal updates

üìê Formatting Guidelines

  • Length: Keep to 4-6 lines maximum
  • Font: Same as email body, avoid fancy formatting
  • Information: Include only essential contact details
  • Legal disclaimers: Follow organizational policy
  • Images: Use sparingly - they increase file size

Mobile Email Considerations

üì± Mobile-Friendly Practices

  • Subject lines: Front-load important information (first 30 characters)
  • Body text: Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)
  • Action items: Put them at the beginning
  • Links: Make them easy to tap
  • Attachments: Mention early in the email
