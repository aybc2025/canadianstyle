10.01 Introduction to Correspondence

Professional correspondence reflects the quality and credibility of government communications. This chapter covers Canadian government standards for letters, memorandums, and other formal correspondence.

Correspondence Principles

  • Clear, concise, and courteous communication
  • Consistent formatting and professional appearance
  • Appropriate tone for audience and purpose
  • Compliance with government standards

Types of Correspondence

  • Business letters: External communication
  • Memorandums: Internal communication
  • Formal notes: Diplomatic correspondence
  • Electronic mail: Digital communication

Government Context

  • Represents the Government of Canada
  • Must follow accessibility standards
  • Subject to access to information laws
  • Reflects public service values

10.02 Business Letters

Business Letter Structure

Government business letters follow a standard format that ensures professional presentation and effective communication.

Standard Business Letter Format:

GOVERNMENT OF CANADA
Department of Justice Canada
284 Wellington Street
Ottawa, Ontario K1A 0H8

March 15, 2025

Ms. Sarah Johnson
Director of Communications
Canadian Bar Association
500 - 865 Carling Avenue
Ottawa, Ontario K1S 5S8

Dear Ms. Johnson:

Thank you for your letter of March 1, 2025, regarding the proposed amendments to the Criminal Code. We appreciate your organization's thoughtful input on this important legislation.

The Department of Justice is carefully reviewing all submissions received during the consultation period. Your recommendations regarding the implementation timeline will be given due consideration as we finalize the regulatory framework.

We will ensure that the Canadian Bar Association receives advance notice of any regulatory changes and implementation schedules. Should you have any questions about this matter, please do not hesitate to contact me directly.

Yours sincerely,

[Signature]

David Thompson
Assistant Deputy Minister
Policy Development Branch

Essential Elements

  • Letterhead: Official government identification
  • Date: Full date in Canadian format
  • Inside address: Complete recipient information
  • Salutation: Appropriate greeting
  • Body: Clear, organized message
  • Closing: Professional sign-off
  • Signature block: Name, title, department

Salutations

  • Formal: Dear Ms. Smith:
  • Unknown name: Dear Sir or Madam:
  • Group: Dear Colleagues:
  • Officials: Dear Minister:

Closings

  • Standard: Yours sincerely,
  • Formal: Yours truly,
  • Respectful: Respectfully yours,
  • Collaborative: Yours in partnership,

10.15 Memorandums

Memorandum Purpose

Memorandums facilitate internal communication within government, providing clear, documented information exchange between departments, branches, and individuals.

Standard Memorandum Format:

MEMORANDUM

TO: Sarah Wilson, Director General, Policy Branch

FROM: Michael Chen, Senior Policy Analyst

DATE: March 15, 2025

SUBJECT: Quarterly Report on Immigration Processing Times

Purpose

This memorandum provides an update on immigration processing times for Q1 2025 and recommends actions to address current delays.

Background

Processing times have increased by 15% compared to Q4 2024, primarily due to increased application volumes and staffing challenges in regional offices.

Current Status

  • Average processing time: 18 months (target: 12 months)
  • Backlog: 45,000 applications
  • Regional variations: Atlantic (22 months), West (14 months)

Recommendations

  1. Increase temporary staffing in Atlantic region
  2. Implement digital processing pilot program
  3. Revise service standards based on current capacity

Next Steps

I will prepare detailed implementation plans for each recommendation and circulate them for review by March 30, 2025.

Memorandum Elements

  • Header: "MEMORANDUM" or "MEMO"
  • TO: Recipient name and title
  • FROM: Sender name and title
  • DATE: Full date
  • SUBJECT: Clear, specific topic
  • Body: Organized content with clear structure

Subject Line Guidelines

  • Specific: "Budget Variance Report - Q1 2025"
  • Action-oriented: "Request for Approval - New Hiring"
  • Informative: "Update on Climate Action Plan"
  • Concise: Avoid unnecessary words

Distribution

  • Primary recipient: In TO field
  • Copies: cc: (carbon copy)
  • Blind copies: bcc: (when appropriate)
  • File copy: For records management

10.25 Formatting Standards

General Formatting Guidelines

Consistent formatting enhances readability and projects a professional image for government correspondence.

Margins and Spacing

  • Margins: 1 inch (2.5 cm) all sides
  • Line spacing: Single-spaced within paragraphs
  • Paragraph spacing: Double space between paragraphs
  • Indentation: None for block format

Font and Typography

  • Font: Times New Roman, Arial, or Calibri
  • Size: 11 or 12 point
  • Emphasis: Bold for headings, minimal italics
  • Consistency: Same font throughout document

Letterhead Requirements

  • Government identifier: "Government of Canada"
  • Department name: Official bilingual name
  • Address: Complete mailing address
  • Contact information: Phone, email, website

Page Layout

  • First page: Official letterhead
  • Subsequent pages: Header with page number
  • Page numbers: Bottom center or top right
  • White space: Adequate margins and spacing

Electronic Format

  • File format: PDF for final versions
  • Accessibility: Tagged PDFs when required
  • Security: Appropriate classification markings
  • Version control: Clear file naming conventions

Bilingual Requirements

For bilingual correspondence:

  • Both languages on letterhead
  • Appropriate language for recipient
  • Translation quality assurance
  • Cultural sensitivity considerations

10.30 Addresses

Address Formatting

Proper address formatting ensures mail delivery and reflects attention to detail in government correspondence.

Canadian Address Format:

Individual:

Ms. Janet Smith
Director of Operations
ABC Corporation
1234 Main Street, Suite 567
Toronto, Ontario M5V 3A8

Government Office:

The Honourable John Doe
Minister of Finance
Department of Finance Canada
90 Elgin Street
Ottawa, Ontario K1A 0G5

International:

Dr. Maria Rodriguez
Institute for Policy Research
1500 K Street NW, Suite 400
Washington, DC 20005
UNITED STATES

Address Elements

  • Name line: Full name with title
  • Title line: Position and organization
  • Street address: Number, street, unit
  • City, province/state: With proper spacing
  • Postal code: Correct format
  • Country: For international mail

Titles and Honorifics

  • Government officials: The Honourable
  • Academic: Dr., Professor
  • Professional: Mr., Ms., Mrs.
  • Military: Colonel, General

Postal Abbreviations

  • Provinces: ON, BC, AB, QC
  • Directions: N, S, E, W, NE, NW
  • Street types: St, Ave, Blvd, Dr
  • Unit types: Apt, Suite, Unit, Floor

Special Considerations

  • Privacy: Protect personal information
  • Accuracy: Verify addresses before sending
  • Updates: Maintain current contact information
  • Accessibility: Consider alternative formats

10.40 Signatures and Sign-offs

Signature Block Components

Professional signature blocks provide clear identification and contact information for recipients.

Standard Signature Block:

Basic Format:

Yours sincerely,

[Handwritten signature]

Sarah M. Johnson
Assistant Deputy Minister
Strategic Policy Branch
Department of Innovation, Science and Economic Development

Tel: 613-555-0123
Email: sarah.johnson@canada.ca

With Delegation:

Yours sincerely,

[Handwritten signature]

Michael Chen
for Sarah M. Johnson
Assistant Deputy Minister
Strategic Policy Branch

Electronic Signatures

  • Digital signatures: Legally recognized authentication
  • Electronic signatures: Scanned or typed names
  • Security: Appropriate for document sensitivity
  • Standards: Government-approved signature systems

Delegation Indicators

  • "for": Acting with authority
  • "per": On behalf of (secretary)
  • "Acting": Temporary appointment
  • "A/": Abbreviated acting designation

Contact Information

  • Telephone: Direct line preferred
  • Email: Official government address
  • Address: If different from letterhead
  • Alternative contact: When appropriate

Signature Authority

  • Delegation: Within assigned authority
  • Approval: Required for significant commitments
  • Recording: Maintain signature cards
  • Security: Protect signature integrity

Special Situations

  • Multiple signatories: Joint approval
  • Copies: "Original signed by"
  • Urgent matters: Telephone confirmation
  • Confidential: Secure handling procedures

Quality Control

  • Review: Verify content before signing
  • Authority: Confirm signing authority
  • Records: Maintain proper files
  • Follow-up: Track response requirements
