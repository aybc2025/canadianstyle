7.01 Introduction

Understanding punctuation as the foundation of clear, professional communication

The Role of Punctuation

Punctuation serves as the traffic signals of language, guiding readers through your ideas and ensuring clarity in government communications.

Punctuation Principles

  • Clarity: Punctuation should always serve to clarify meaning
  • Consistency: Use punctuation consistently throughout your document
  • Standard usage: Follow established Canadian conventions
  • Reader-focused: Consider how punctuation affects reader comprehension

Impact of Punctuation on Meaning

Clear: The minister said the report was accurate.
Different meaning: "The minister," said the report, "was accurate."
Ambiguous: The minister said the report was accurate according to officials.

7.03 Commas

Master the complex rules governing comma usage

Essential Comma Rules

  • Series: Separate items in a series (Oxford comma recommended)
  • Independent clauses: Before coordinating conjunctions
  • Non-essential elements: Set off parenthetical information
  • Introductory elements: After introductory phrases

Examples
