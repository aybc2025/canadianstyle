4.01 General Principles

üìè Foundation of Good Writing

Proper capitalization enhances readability and shows attention to detail in professional writing.

Basic Capitalization Rules

Always Capitalize

  • The first word of a sentence
  • Proper nouns (names of specific people, places, things)
  • The pronoun "I"
  • First word of a direct quotation

Sometimes Capitalize

  • Titles when they precede names
  • Directions when referring to regions
  • Common nouns in specific contexts
  • Job titles in formal addresses

Examples in Context

Correct: "Prime Minister Trudeau announced the policy."

Incorrect: "prime minister trudeau announced the Policy."

4.02 Proper Nouns

Proper nouns name specific people, places, organizations, and unique things. They are always capitalized.

Categories of Proper Nouns

  • Personal Names: John Smith, Mary Johnson
  • Place Names: Toronto, Lake Ontario, Rocky Mountains
  • Organizations: Parliament of Canada, United Nations
  • Brand Names: Microsoft, Coca-Cola, iPhone
  • Historical Events: World War II, Confederation

4.03 Titles & Positions

Capitalize titles when they directly precede a person's name or when used in formal address.

Capitalization Rules for Titles

  • Before names: President Biden, Doctor Smith
  • In formal address: "Welcome, Mr. President"
  • After names: Generally lowercase unless very formal
  • Standing alone: Usually lowercase unless referring to specific office

4.04 Organizations

Capitalize the names of specific organizations, institutions, and government bodies.

Organization Types

  • Government Bodies: House of Commons, Supreme Court of Canada
  • Educational Institutions: McGill University, Seneca College
  • Companies: Air Canada, Canadian Tire Corporation
  • Non-profits: Red Cross, United Way

Specific vs. Generic References

Specific: "The University of British Columbia announced..."

Generic: "Most universities require..."

4.05 Academic Subjects

Generally, academic subjects are lowercase unless they are proper nouns or part of a specific course title.

Subject Capitalization

  • Lowercase: mathematics, history, physics, psychology
  • Capitalize: English, French, Spanish (languages)
  • Course Titles: Introduction to Psychology 101
  • Department Names: Department of Biology

Academic Context Examples

"She studied biology and French literature."

"The English Department offers creative writing courses."

4.06 Special Cases

Some capitalization situations require special attention and may vary based on context or style guide preferences.

Special Capitalization Situations

  • Seasons: Generally lowercase (spring, summer, fall, winter)
  • Directions: Capitalize when referring to regions (the West, Eastern Canada)
  • Internet/Web: Follow current conventions
  • Hyphenated Words: Capitalize both parts if both are normally capitalized

Special Cases in Practice

Seasons: "We'll meet this fall" (lowercase)

Regions: "Economic growth in the West" (capitalized)

Directions: "Drive west on Highway 1" (lowercase)
