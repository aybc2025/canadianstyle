4.01 Introduction

üìè Foundation of Good Writing

Proper capitalization enhances readability and shows attention to detail in professional writing.

Basic Principles

  • First words: Capitalize the first word of sentences
  • Proper nouns: Names of specific people, places, organizations
  • Titles: When used with names or in formal contexts
  • Consistency: Follow the same style throughout your document

4.02 General Principles

Understanding the fundamental rules of capitalization provides the foundation for all other applications.

Core Capitalization Rules

  • Sentence beginnings: Always capitalize the first word
  • Proper nouns: Specific names require capitals
  • Common nouns: Generally lowercase unless they begin sentences
  • Adjectives from proper nouns: Usually capitalized (Canadian, Shakespearean)

4.03 Proper Nouns

Proper nouns name specific people, places, things, or concepts and are always capitalized.

Always Capitalize

  • People's names: John Smith, Marie Curie, William Shakespeare
  • Geographical names: Canada, Toronto, Lake Ontario, Rocky Mountains
  • Organizations: United Nations, Parliament of Canada
  • Events: World War II, Canada Day

Proper Noun Examples

‚úì Person: "Prime Minister Trudeau spoke today."

‚úì Place: "The meeting was held in Ottawa."

‚úì Organization: "Statistics Canada released new data."

4.04 Titles & Positions

Job titles and positions require careful attention to capitalization rules.

Capitalize When

  • Before names: Minister Jones, Professor Smith
  • In formal address: "Thank you, Minister."
  • In lists of officials: The Prime Minister, Deputy Minister

Don't Capitalize When

  • Generic references: "The minister announced..."
  • Job descriptions: "She works as a manager."
  • Plural forms: "All deputy ministers attended."

4.05 Organizations

Organization names follow specific capitalization patterns.

Government Organizations

  • Full names: Department of National Defence
  • Common abbreviations: DND (when well-known)
  • Generic terms: the department, the ministry

4.07 Publications & Titles

Publication titles and works of art follow specific capitalization conventions in Canadian English.

Publication Title Rules

  • Major words: Capitalize all significant words
  • Articles, conjunctions, prepositions: Lowercase unless first/last word
  • First and last words: Always capitalize
  • Words after colons: Capitalize first word after colon

4.08 Special Cases

Certain situations require special attention to capitalization rules, including holidays, directions, and unique circumstances.

Always Capitalize

  • Holidays: Canada Day, Christmas, New Year's Day
  • Historical periods: Renaissance, Industrial Revolution
  • Awards: Nobel Prize, Order of Canada
  • Brand names: Kleenex, Xerox (but not generic use)

Directional Guidelines

  • Regions: Northern Ontario, the West (when referring to Western Canada)
  • Directions: head north, travel east (lowercase for simple directions)
  • Streets: Bay Street, Main Street West
