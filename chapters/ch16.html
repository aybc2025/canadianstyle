16.01 Introduction

Effective revision and proofreading are essential for producing high-quality government documents. This systematic approach ensures accuracy, clarity, and professionalism in all written communications.

Revision Levels

  • Structural Revision: Organization, flow, content completeness
  • Line Editing: Sentence structure, word choice, tone
  • Copy Editing: Grammar, punctuation, spelling, style
  • Proofreading: Final error detection, format verification

Key Principles

  • Multiple passes: Review documents several times, focusing on different aspects each time
  • Time gap: Allow time between writing and revising for objectivity
  • Systematic approach: Use checklists to ensure comprehensive review
  • Purpose focus: Always consider the document's intended purpose and audience

16.02 Levels of Editing

Different editing levels address specific aspects of document quality. Understanding these levels helps you focus your efforts and ensure comprehensive review.

Structural Editing

Focus on overall organization, logic, and completeness.

  • Assess document organization and flow
  • Verify completeness of content
  • Check logical progression of arguments
  • Ensure consistency with document purpose
  • Review headings and subheadings for hierarchy

Copy Editing

Address grammar, punctuation, style, and language usage.

  • Correct grammar and syntax errors
  • Fix punctuation and capitalization
  • Correct spelling and typographical errors
  • Ensure consistent application of style guidelines
  • Verify proper citation and reference formatting

Proofreading

Final review for surface errors and formatting consistency.

  • Catch remaining spelling and typographical errors
  • Verify page numbers and cross-references
  • Check formatting consistency (fonts, spacing, alignment)
  • Review final layout and print quality

16.03 Revision Strategies

Effective revision requires systematic approaches and proven techniques. These strategies help ensure thorough, efficient review processes.

The Multi-Pass Approach

  • Pass 1 - Content and Structure: Review overall organization, completeness, and logical flow
  • Pass 2 - Style and Clarity: Focus on sentence structure, word choice, and clarity
  • Pass 3 - Grammar and Mechanics: Address grammar, punctuation, spelling, and formatting
  • Pass 4 - Final Polish: Catch remaining errors and ensure consistency

Time Management Strategies

  • Plan revision time: Allow adequate time for thorough review
  • Take breaks: Fresh eyes catch more errors
  • Set priorities: Focus on most critical issues first
  • Use deadlines: Work backwards from submission dates

16.04 Proofreading Techniques

Proofreading requires specific techniques to catch errors that previous editing passes may have missed.

Reading Techniques

  • Read aloud: Hear errors you might miss visually
  • Read backwards: Start from the end to focus on spelling
  • Use a ruler: Cover text below the line you're reading
  • Change format: Print or use different screen view

Focus Areas

  • Commonly confused words: their/there/they're, its/it's
  • Number formatting: Consistency in dates, amounts
  • Proper names: Spellings of people and places
  • Technical terms: Industry-specific vocabulary

16.05 Common Errors

Recognizing common error patterns helps focus your revision efforts on areas most likely to contain mistakes.

Language and Usage Errors

  • Subject-verb agreement: Singular/plural mismatches
  • Pronoun reference: Unclear or incorrect pronouns
  • Sentence fragments: Incomplete thoughts
  • Run-on sentences: Multiple ideas improperly joined
  • Modifier placement: Dangling or misplaced modifiers

Usage and Gallicisms

Canadian English documents often contain French influences that require attention during revision:

  • French word placement: Incorrect positioning of French terms
  • False friends: French words with different English meanings
  • Gallicisms: French sentence structures in English
  • Mixed language usage: Inconsistent treatment of bilingual content
  • Accent marks: Missing or incorrect French accents
  • Italicization: Improper formatting of French words

Specialized Resource: For comprehensive guidance on identifying and correcting French-English usage issues, see Appendix A: French Words in English Text.

Mechanical Errors

  • Spelling: Especially Canadian vs. American spellings
  • Punctuation: Comma splices, apostrophe misuse
  • Capitalization: Inconsistent application of rules
  • Number style: Mixed formats for dates, amounts

16.06 Technical Accuracy

Technical accuracy goes beyond language mechanics to ensure factual correctness and proper formatting of specialized content.

Technical Verification Points

  • Factual accuracy: Verify statistics, dates, and claims
  • Citations: Check completeness and formatting
  • Cross-references: Ensure internal links are accurate
  • Technical terminology: Verify specialized vocabulary
  • Units and measurements: Check for consistency
  • URLs and contact information: Test all links and verify details

16.07 Master Revision Checklist

Use this comprehensive checklist to ensure thorough revision of your documents.

Content and Structure

  • All required content included
  • Logical flow and organization
  • Document purpose clearly communicated
  • Content appropriate for target audience

Language and Style

  • Plain language principles followed
  • Uniform vocabulary throughout
  • Bias-free and inclusive language
  • French-English usage issues addressed (see Appendix A)
  • Tone consistent and appropriate

Grammar and Mechanics

  • Grammar and syntax correct
  • Spelling verified (Canadian spellings)
  • Punctuation accurate and consistent
  • Capitalization consistent
  • French words properly formatted (italics, accents)

Technical Elements

  • Number formatting consistent
  • Dates accurate and properly formatted
  • Citations complete and properly formatted
  • Cross-references and page numbers accurate

Format and Layout

  • Heading styles consistent
  • Font usage consistent
  • Spacing and alignment consistent
  • Headers, footers, and page numbers correct

16.08 Final Review

The final review ensures your document meets all quality standards before publication or submission.

Complete Document Review

Read the entire document once more, focusing on overall flow and reader experience.

Spot Check Critical Elements

  • Title page accuracy
  • Table of contents alignment
  • Executive summary consistency
  • Key statistics and data
  • Contact information

Final Quality Assurance

  • Document serves its intended purpose
  • All stakeholder requirements met
  • Accessibility standards followed
  • Legal and policy compliance
  • Ready for distribution
