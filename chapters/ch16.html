16.02 Levels of Editing

Different editing levels address specific aspects of document quality. Understanding when and how to apply each level ensures thorough revision.

üìã Structural Editing (Substantive Editing)

Focuses on content organization, logic, and completeness.

Structural Editing Tasks:

  • Evaluate overall document structure and organization
  • Ensure logical flow between sections and paragraphs
  • Check completeness of content against stated objectives
  • Verify that conclusions follow from evidence presented
  • Assess whether the document meets its intended purpose
  • Identify gaps in information or argumentation
  • Recommend reorganization or additional content as needed

‚úèÔ∏è Line Editing (Stylistic Editing)

Improves clarity, tone, and readability at the sentence and paragraph level.

Line Editing Tasks:

  • Improve sentence structure and variety
  • Enhance word choice for precision and clarity
  • Ensure consistent tone throughout document
  • Eliminate wordiness and redundancy
  • Strengthen transitions between ideas
  • Adjust reading level for target audience
  • Improve paragraph unity and coherence

üìù Copy Editing

Corrects grammar, punctuation, spelling, and ensures style consistency.

Copy Editing Tasks:

  • Correct grammar and syntax errors
  • Fix punctuation and capitalization
  • Correct spelling and typographical errors
  • Ensure consistent application of style guidelines
  • Verify proper citation and reference formatting
  • Check number and date formatting consistency
  • Ensure appropriate use of abbreviations and acronyms

üîç Proofreading

Final review for surface errors and formatting consistency.

Proofreading Tasks:

  • Catch remaining spelling and typographical errors
  • Verify page numbers and cross-references
  • Check formatting consistency (fonts, spacing, alignment)
  • Ensure proper placement of headers, footers, and page elements
  • Verify accuracy of table of contents and indexes
  • Check image placement and captions
  • Review final layout and print quality

16.03 Revision Strategies

Effective revision requires systematic approaches and proven techniques. These strategies help ensure thorough and efficient document review.

üîÑ Multiple Pass Strategy
  • First pass: Overall structure and content
  • Second pass: Paragraph and sentence level
  • Third pass: Grammar and punctuation
  • Final pass: Formatting and layout
üëÅÔ∏è Fresh Eyes Technique
  • Take breaks between writing and revising
  • Read aloud to catch errors
  • Have colleagues review important documents
  • Use text-to-speech software
üéØ Focused Reading
  • Read for one type of error at a time
  • Use ruler or paper to focus on individual lines
  • Read backwards for spelling errors
  • Check numbers and dates separately
üìä Systematic Approach
  • Use comprehensive checklists
  • Track common error patterns
  • Maintain personal style sheets
  • Document revision decisions

Advanced Revision Techniques

üîç Reverse Reading Method

Read the document from end to beginning, sentence by sentence. This technique helps identify:

  • Spelling errors that spell-check might miss
  • Inconsistent formatting
  • Repeated words or phrases
  • Typography issues

üìã Checklist Integration

Develop and use specialized checklists for different document types:

  • Reports: Executive summary accuracy, data verification
  • Letters: Proper salutations, consistent tone
  • Policies: Clear directive language, implementation steps
  • Technical documents: Terminology consistency, procedure accuracy

üìä Data & Statistics

  2. Verify mathematical calculations independently
  3. Check data visualization accuracy (charts, graphs)
  4. Confirm sample sizes and methodology descriptions
  5. Validate confidence intervals and significance levels
  6. Ensure consistent decimal precision throughout
