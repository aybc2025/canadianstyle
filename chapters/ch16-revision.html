16.01 Introduction

Good writing is rewriting. Professional documents require multiple levels of revision to ensure clarity, accuracy, and effectiveness.

The Four Levels of Editing

1. Structural Editing

Organization, content, and overall approach

2. Stylistic Editing

Clarity, flow, and tone

3. Copy Editing

Grammar, spelling, and consistency

4. Proofreading

Final check for errors and formatting

üí° Best Practice

Allow time between writing and editing. Fresh eyes catch more errors and see opportunities for improvement.

16.02 Structural Editing

Structural editing examines the document's foundation: its purpose, audience, and organization.

Key Questions for Structural Editing

  • Is the purpose clear and consistently maintained?
  • Is the content appropriate for the intended audience?
  • Is the information complete and accurate?
  • Is the organization logical and effective?
  • Are the main points clearly stated and well-supported?

Common Structural Issues

Problem Solution
Unclear purpose Add a clear statement of purpose early in the document
Missing information Research and add necessary content
Poor organization Reorganize using logical order (chronological, spatial, importance)
Weak conclusions Strengthen ending with clear recommendations or summary
Structural Editing in Action

Before: Report jumps between topics without clear transitions

After: Report follows logical progression with smooth transitions between related topics

16.03 Stylistic Editing

Stylistic editing improves clarity, flow, and readability while maintaining the author's voice.

Areas of Focus

  • Clarity: Eliminate ambiguity and confusion
  • Conciseness: Remove unnecessary words
  • Flow: Ensure smooth transitions
  • Tone: Match the audience and purpose
  • Voice: Maintain consistency throughout

16.04 Copy Editing

Copy editing ensures correctness and consistency in grammar, spelling, punctuation, and formatting.

Copy Editing Checklist

Grammar & Usage

  • Subject-verb agreement
  • Pronoun references
  • Parallel structure
  • Modifier placement

Spelling & Punctuation

  • Consistent Canadian spelling
  • Proper comma usage
  • Correct apostrophes
  • Appropriate capitalization

Consistency

  • Number format
  • Date format
  • Abbreviations
  • Terminology

References

  • Citation format
  • Footnote style
  • Bibliography entries
  • Cross-references

üîç Copy Editing Tips

  • Read slowly and carefully
  • Check one type of error at a time
  • Use style sheets for consistency
  • Don't rely solely on spell-checkers

16.05 Proofreading

Proofreading is the final quality check before publication, focusing on surface errors and formatting.

Proofreading Focus Areas

  • Typographical errors
  • Formatting consistency
  • Page layout issues
  • Broken links or references
  • Header and footer accuracy
  • Table of contents accuracy

Effective Proofreading Techniques

Read Backwards

Read from end to beginning to catch spelling errors

Change Format

Print the document or change the font to see it fresh

Read Aloud

Speaking reveals awkward phrasing and missing words

Use a Ruler

Guide your eyes line by line to maintain focus

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Proofreading Pitfalls

  • Reading too quickly
  • Proofreading on screen only
  • Proofreading immediately after writing
  • Ignoring formatting and layout
