6.01 Basic Principles

Italics provide visual emphasis and distinguish special text from regular content. Use them strategically for maximum impact.

When to Use Italics

  • Emphasis: To stress important words or phrases
  • Titles: Books, newspapers, films, and other major works
  • Foreign words: Non-English terms not yet adopted
  • Technical terms: When first introduced or defined
  • Legal references: Case names and legislation

6.02 Emphasis

Italics can emphasize key words or phrases, but should be used judiciously to maintain their impact.

Emphasis Guidelines

  • Sparingly: Only for truly important emphasis
  • Alternative methods: Consider sentence structure, word choice
  • Avoid overuse: Too many italics diminish effectiveness
  • Consistency: Use same approach throughout document

Effective Emphasis

‚úì Strategic emphasis: "The deadline is tomorrow, not next week."

‚úó Overuse: "All employees must submit their reports immediately."

‚úì Better alternative: "All employees must submit their reports immediately."

üí° Professional Tip

In professional writing, strong sentence structure and precise word choice often provide better emphasis than italics.

6.03 Foreign Words & Phrases

Foreign words and phrases that haven't been fully adopted into English are typically italicized.

Foreign Word Guidelines

  • Italicize: Words not yet adopted into English
  • Roman type: Words commonly used in English
  • Latin terms: Scientific and legal Latin is usually italicized
  • Check dictionaries: For adoption status

Different types of published works require different formatting - some use italics, others use quotation marks.

Titles in Italics

  • Newspapers: The Globe and Mail
  • Magazines: Maclean's
  • Films/TV series: Anne of Green Gables
  • Albums/CDs: Tragically Hip's Day for Night

6.05 Technical & Scientific Terms

Technical and scientific writing uses italics for specific conventions including species names and introduced terms.

Scientific Conventions

  • Species names: Homo sapiens, Quercus alba
  • Genus names: Escherichia (in E. coli)
  • Mathematical variables: x, y, n
  • Statistical notation: p value, F statistic

Technical Term Introduction

  • First mention: Italicize when defining new terms
  • Subsequent use: Regular roman type
  • Key concepts: Can be italicized throughout for emphasis

Scientific & Technical Examples

üß¨ Biological Sciences
  • The species Canis lupus (gray wolf)
  • E. coli bacteria can cause illness
  • The genus Rosa includes all roses
üî¨ Research & Statistics
  • The p value was less than 0.05
  • Calculate the mean (M) and standard deviation
  • The F ratio indicates significance
üíª Technology
  • The process of machine learning involves...
  • Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly evolving
  • The term blockchain refers to...

6.06 Legal References

Legal writing has specific conventions for italicizing case names, legislation, and legal terms.

Legal Citation Rules

  • Case names: R. v. Smith, Brown v. Board
  • Legislation: Charter of Rights and Freedoms
  • Short forms: Roman type ("the Act", "the Charter")
  • Legal terms: prima facie, res judicata
