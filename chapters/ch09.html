9.01 Introduction

Reference matter provides readers with the sources, explanations, and additional information they need to understand and verify your work. They provide detailed information that some readers need while keeping the main text focused.

Types of Reference Matter

  • Footnotes: Brief explanations at bottom of pages
  • Endnotes: Detailed notes at end of sections or document
  • Bibliography: Complete list of sources consulted
  • Index: Alphabetical guide to document contents
  • Appendices: Supporting documents and data

Reference Matter Organization

Document structure:

  • Main text with footnotes
  • Appendices (A, B, C, etc.)
  • Bibliography
  • Index

9.02 Footnotes

Footnotes provide immediate access to additional information without interrupting the flow of the main text.

Footnote Guidelines

  • Numbering: Use superscript numbers¬π
  • Placement: Bottom of the page
  • Length: Keep brief - under half a page
  • Format: Smaller font, separated by line

9.08 Appendices & Reference Materials

Appendices contain supplementary material that supports the main document but would interrupt the flow if included in the body text.

What to Include in Appendices

  • Raw data: Detailed statistics, survey results
  • Supporting documents: Legislation, regulations, policies
  • Technical details: Methodology, calculations, specifications
  • Supplementary information: Background material, historical data
  • Language guides: Style rules, formatting guidelines

Appendix Organization

Typical appendix structure:

  • Appendix A: Survey Questionnaire
  • Appendix B: Statistical Analysis
  • Appendix C: Stakeholder Responses
  • Appendix D: Budget Calculations
  • Appendix E: Legislative References

Appendix Guidelines

  • Reference in text: Direct readers to relevant appendices
  • Logical order: Arrange in order of mention in main text
  • Clear titles: Descriptive headings for each appendix
  • Self-contained: Each appendix should make sense alone

Referencing Appendices

In main text:

  • "Detailed survey results are provided in Appendix A."
  • "For French capitalization rules, see Appendix A."
  • "Budget calculations (Appendix D) support these recommendations."
