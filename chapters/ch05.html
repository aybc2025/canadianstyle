5

Numerical Expressions

Clear and consistent presentation of numbers in your writing

5.01 Introduction 5.02 Round Numbers 5.03 Consistency 5.04 Beginning Sentences 5.05 Quantities 5.06 Money 5.07 Percentages 5.08 Time 5.09 Dates

5.01 Introduction

Clear presentation of numerical information improves document readability and professionalism. This chapter provides guidelines for consistent use of numbers in government writing.

General Principles

  • Be consistent within a document
  • Consider your audience and context
  • Use numerals for technical and scientific writing
  • Write out numbers for formal or literary texts
  • Always spell out numbers that begin sentences

5.02 Round Numbers

The Ten Rule

Write out numbers from one to nine; use numerals for 10 and above:

‚úÖ Correct

three employees
15 departments
seven years
12 months

‚ùå Avoid

3 employees
fifteen departments
7 years
twelve months

Exceptions to the Ten Rule

Use Numerals for:

  • Technical contexts: 5 metres, 8 kilograms
  • Ages: 5 years old, under 9
  • Statistics: 3 out of 4 respondents
  • Page references: page 7, chapter 3
  • Lists with mixed numbers: 3 cars, 15 trucks, and 22 buses

5.03 Consistency

Parallel Structure

When numbers appear in parallel contexts, treat them consistently:

‚úÖ Consistent

The department has 5 managers, 12 supervisors, and 18 staff members.

‚ùå Inconsistent

The department has five managers, 12 supervisors, and 18 staff members.

Series and Lists

In a series containing any number 10 or higher, use numerals for all:

Examples

  • We need 3, 8, and 15 copies respectively
  • The results were 4 positive, 12 negative, and 22 inconclusive
  • Chapters 2, 5, and 7 require revision

5.04 Numbers at the Beginning of Sentences

Always Spell Out

Never begin a sentence with numerals. Always spell out numbers that start sentences:

‚úÖ Correct

Fifteen people attended the meeting.
Two hundred applications were received.
Forty-five percent agreed with the proposal.

‚ùå Avoid

15 people attended the meeting.
200 applications were received.
45% agreed with the proposal.

Alternative Constructions

For large numbers that would be awkward to spell out, rewrite the sentence:

Better Alternatives

  • Instead of: "1,250 employees work here"
  • Write: "The office employs 1,250 people"
  • Or: "There are 1,250 employees here"

5.05 Quantities and Measurements

Units of Measurement

Use numerals with units of measurement, regardless of the ten rule:

‚úÖ Measurements

5 metres
3 kilometres
8 kilograms
2 litres

‚úÖ Scientific Units

7 degrees Celsius
4 hectares
9 cubic metres
6 millilitres

Approximations

For round numbers used as approximations, you may spell them out:

Examples

  • about a hundred people
  • several thousand dollars
  • a few million citizens
  • thousands of applications

5.06 Money

Currency Formats

Use numerals for specific amounts; spell out general references:

‚úÖ Specific Amounts

$25
$1,500
$3.50
$2.5 million

‚úÖ General References

a few dollars
several thousand dollars
millions of dollars
about five dollars

Large Amounts

Guidelines

  • Millions: $2.5 million (not $2,500,000)
  • Billions: $1.2 billion
  • Cents only: 75 cents (not $0.75 in text)
  • Even dollars: $25 (not $25.00 in text)

5.07 Percentages

Format

Use numerals with the percent symbol (%) in technical writing, spell out in formal text:

‚úÖ Technical/Scientific

25%
3.5%
100%
0.25%

‚úÖ Formal Writing

twenty-five percent
three and a half percent
one hundred percent
less than one percent

Special Cases

Guidelines

  • Beginning sentences: Always spell out
  • In tables: Always use %
  • Fractional percentages: 2.5% or two and a half percent
  • Range: 15% to 20% or 15-20%

5.08 Time

Clock Time

Use numerals for specific times; spell out general references:

‚úÖ Specific Times

9:00 a.m.
2:30 p.m.
12:00 noon
12:00 midnight

‚úÖ General References

about nine o'clock
half past two
early morning
late afternoon

Periods of Time

Guidelines

  • Ages: 5 years old, a five-year-old child
  • Decades: the 1990s, the nineties
  • Centuries: 21st century, twentieth century
  • Duration: 3 hours, three-hour meeting

5.09 Dates

Canadian Format

Use the international standard format (YYYY-MM-DD) for official documents:

‚úÖ Official Documents

2024-03-15
2024-12-01
2023-07-04

‚úÖ Narrative Text

March 15, 2024
December 1, 2024
July 4, 2023

Special Formats

Examples

  • Years only: 2024, the year 2024
  • Months and years: March 2024, March of 2024
  • Ranges: 2023-2024, 2023 to 2024
  • Academic years: 2024-2025 academic year
  • Historical: July 1, 1867 (Confederation)
  • Decades: the 1960s, early 2000s, mid-1990s
